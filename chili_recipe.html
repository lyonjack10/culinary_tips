The Best Chili You Will Ever Eat


Ingredients


  • San Marzano Tomatoes (Whole Peeled)
  • Tri-blend Beans
  • 1 lb of 80/20 Ground Beef
  • Spring Onions
  • 8-12 oz Ribeye
  • Shredded Chedder Cheese
  • Poblano Peppers
  • 3-4 Cloves of Garlic
  • 2 Jalapenos
  • Sour Cream
  • Avocado Oil
  • Spice Mix (store bought or your own)
  • High Heat Oil

Preparation


  1. To start you have to first decide if you want to use a store bought spice mix or a spice mix of your own. I personally always use my own and it consists of salt, pepper, onion powder, garlic powder, red pepper flakes, chili powder, smoked paprika, and chipotle chili powder.
  2. Next, open your tomatoes and break them up into a large pot. Strain and rinse your beans if you would like and add them to the pot with the tomatoes. Let these simmer while you prepare your other ingredients.
  3. Finely chop your onions, jalapenos, garlic, and poblano peppers. Remove the seeds from the peppers if you do not want a spicy chili. Once these are chopped add everything to the pot except the onions, those will be saved for serving.
  4. At this point you can prepare the ground beef. Salt and pepper the ground beef to taste and place in a skillet on medium heat and cook all the way through. You will want to cook until all the pink is gone. Once done, strain the excess juices and add the ground beef to the pot. Make sure to stir the chili as you cook.
  5. The ribeye is optional, but it adds a level of flavor and presentation that is unmatched. Wipe clean the same skillet you used for the ground beef. Salt, pepper, and add avocado oil (or your preferred oil) onto the ribeye. Depending on the thickness of the steak it could take 2-5 minutes per side to cook to medium, medium rare. Use a thermometer to check the temperature or you can always cut into the steak to visually check.
  6. Once your steak is done cooking, pull it from the skillet and let it rest for 2-3 minutes. From there, cut across the grain into bite size chunks.
  7. Finally you can build. Scoop the chili into a bowl, add some of the ribeye chunks to the top, add cheese, spring onions, and sour cream if you would like.
